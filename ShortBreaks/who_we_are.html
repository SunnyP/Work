ACTIVITIES IN ACTION

Text describing the consortium and its goals

The VCS (Voluntary and Community Sector) Consortia provides a range of Short Breaks Services in Luton Borough, to give over five hundred disabled children and young people in the area enjoyable experiences away from their primary carers.

 

This gives those children and young people the chance for personal and social development, while giving their families and carers valuable – and necessary – time off from their caring responsibilities.

London Luton Airport

 

London Luton Airport Limited (LLAL) supports the provision and development of a range of Short Breaks Services in Luton Borough through their Community Partnership Fund, a part of their Corporate Social Responsibility Programme.

 

In partnership with Luton Borough Council (LBC) and the VCS Consortia LLAL strives through funding, direction and support to improve the quality of life for the children and young people of Luton with disabilities and learning difficulties.

 

VCS Consortia membership currently is made up of these organisations:

Autism Bedfordshire

Centre for Youth and Community Development (CYCD)

Dimensions

Families United Network (FUN)

Friends Of Bright Eyes (FOBE)

Hart Hill Adventure Playground (HHAP)

Neet Teens

Phab Frendz

Rainbow Special Needs Playscheme

Special Needs Out Of School Club (SNOOSC)

 

The VCS Consortia is managed by Autism Bedfordshire, whose functions include development of strategic and operational systems, funding allocation, service level agreement management, collation of performance management data and undertaking service reviews.

OUTCOMES

Participation by Parents /  Carers, and Children and Young People

 

Partnership is key to the quality of the Short Breaks Services provided by the VCS Consortia.

 

With the participation of parents and carers, and that of children and young people, the Consortia has been able to share resources, knowledge, strategies and information of all types to support Luton Borough Council and, ultimately, the families depending on the Council for the assistance they require.

 

 

Participation by Parents and Carers

Independent, voluntary and parent-led, the Luton Parent Carer Forum decides on its own priorities and how it would like to be involved in the decision making process when it comes to services for their children in Luton.

 

The Forum receives national support from the National Network of Parent Carer Forums, facilitating feedback from parents and carers across the country.

 

 

Participation by Children and Young People

Defined as a right by the United Nations, and recommended as good practice by Central Government, participation by children and young people in the design and development of services provided for them enaxsures those services are entirely appropriate for their needs.

 

 

Luton Borough Council

In conjunction with the VCS Consortia, Luton Borough Council provides parents and carers with different types of short breaks, including:

 

Outreach support services – regular visits to homes of children or young people

Activity services – taking children or young people swimming, shopping, to the cinema and other activities away from home

Shared care – looking after children or young people in the carer’s own home for a night or a weekend

Domiciliary care – care for children or young people in their own homes

We have identified certain strategic outcomes which we will be updating here on this page on an annual basis.

 

Outcomes for children and young people with disabilities will include the improved ability to:

 

• Experience new activities

• Develop self esteem and confidence

• Become more independent

• Have increased opportunities to socialise with other children

 

Outcomes for siblings will include the improved ability to:

 

• Receive more attention from parents and carers

• Enjoy more time with their friends

• Have more opportunities for a break away from their disabled brother or sister

Outcomes for their parents and carers will include the improved ability to care for their children and:

 

• Enjoy more opportunities to rest, relax and recuperate

• Catch up on what needs to be done in and around the home

• Focus on partner relationships

• Return to – or maintain – education

• Return to – or maintain – employment

 

Outcomes for the Local Authority will include:

 

• A reduced need for social care intervention

• A fall in the number of children and young people with disabilities entering the looked after system

• A decrease in the number of out-of–area placements
